About ORE

Open Research Europe (ORE) is an open access publishing platform established in 2021 to support researchers funded by the European Commission's Horizon 2020 and Horizon Europe programs. The platform provides a venue for publishing research outputs—including articles, data, and software—in an open, transparent manner with open peer review. It aims to accelerate scientific communication and ensure publicly funded research is freely accessible.

2026 will be the year of a significant evolution of the platform, evolving as a collaborative, non-profit initiative. This new phase expands ORE beyond its original European Commission funding model to become a collective effort supported by 10 national funders and research institutions across Europe, alongside continued European Commission involvement.

The new version of ORE will maintain the platform's core commitment to open access and open peer review while broadening its scope in important ways. Publishing eligibility will expand beyond European Commission-funded researchers, making the platform accessible to a wider research community. The platform will also progressively align with diamond open access principles as defined in the 'Action Plan for Diamond Open Access', ensuring research is published without fees for either authors or readers.

CERN will serve as the operating and hosting organization, providing the technical, operational, and administrative infrastructure necessary for the platform's daily management.

12 DEC 2025

CERN Council approves Open Research Europe being hosted by CERN

In December 2025, the CERN Council approved CERN’s role as the hosting and operating organisation for Open Research Europe (ORE), a non-profit, open access scientific publishing platform initiated by the European Commission.
